Some words resemble the Spanish language but they differ in meaning. These words are called false cognates.#
Example:  Actually, depression is becoming a huge problem in our society.#
Correction: Nowadays, depression is becoming a huge problem in our society.#
Note: Actually means in fact, truly.
Some synonyms or close words you can use instead of nowadays are: currently, recently, in recent times, today, lately, now.#

DIVPREGUNTAS
Avoid redundancy in meaning.#
Example: If all of us cooperate together, we will succeed.#
Correction: If all of us cooperate (or work together), we will succeed.#
Note: In this sentence, the words cooperate and together have been used; but these words convey the same meaning.#

DIVPREGUNTAS
Be careful when using adjectives ending in ED / ING because they totally change the meaning of your sentence.#
Example: (if you want to say someone is ashamed) Steve is embarrassing.#
Correction: (if you want to say someone is ashamed) Steve is embarrassed.#
Note: Adjectives ending in -ed describe emotions while adjectives ending in -ing describe a characteristic of something or someone.#
DIVPREGUNTAS
Another and other are used in different ways. Other is an adjective meaning 'different' and it is used with both, singular and plural nouns; another expresses quantity, something extra or additional and it is used with singular nouns only.#
Example: Have the another cup of tea, not this cup.#
Correction: Have the other cup of tea, not this cup.#
Note: null#
DIVPREGUNTAS
Coherence is the unifying element in good writing. It is what gives a piece of writing its flow. When writing lacks coherence, the reader is forced to stop and reread. Organize your ideas, please.#
Example: What is your name? I live in Armenia.#
Correction: What is your name? I'm jesus.#
Note: null#
DIVPREGUNTAS
Be careful with the use of phrasal verbs (a combination of a verb and preposition, a verb and an adverb, or a verb with both an adverb and a preposition); they can totally change the meaning of your sentence.#
Example: Don't work out. Work inside the house!#
Correction: Don't work outside; work inside the house!#
Note: Work out means to exercise.#
